A1--阳关三叠 A parting tune with a thrice repeated refrain

Introduction

This is a Chinese Art Song. The lyrics are repeated thrice with little changes in each time with different emotions.

The lyrics are from a famous poem in Tang Dynasty(701 D.C.)

The general meanings are as follows:

Morning rain in Wei City wets dust thin.
Refreshing willow twigs and the green inn.
O, friend, please drink another cup of wine.
No one greets you at west of the borderline.

Performance

This piece lasts for 5'10''.

A2-- Parto, parto

Introduction

This is an Aria from Act I in La clemenza di Tito (KV 621). Composed by Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart. This aria is for the Alto with an extensive range, and the variation of speed is very dramatic.

The meanings of the lyrics are as follows:
I go, I go, but, my dearest, make peace again with me. I will be what you would most have me be, do whatever you wish.
Look at me, and I will forget all and fly to avenge you; I will think only of that glance at me.
Ah, ye gods, what power you have given beauty!

Performance

This piece lasts for 5'41''. Cadanza starts at about 4'21''

A3--The Hun-Duan Mountain

Introduction

This is a segment in a Chinese Folk Song for Alto, chorus and orchestra. The tunes are from Guizhou Province in Chinese pentatonic scales. To match the accompniments, the voice I used is a little thinner than my usual voice.

The meanings of the lyrics are as follows:
The mountain road of Hun-Duan Mountain is really rugged.
The sky seems to be like fire, and the water seems to be like silver.

Performance

This piece lasts for 2'03''.
