Teacher

This page shows some of my thoughts from my music learning and teaching. All the articles are available through the links on the titles.

T1--Some thoughts on reading the scores

It seems impossible for an adult beginner to read the full orchestra scores. However, according to my experiences of both learning and pedagogy, there are some tips which may give you some food for thought.

T2--Some thoughts on ear training

Not everybody has an absolute pitch (fortunately I have one), but relative pitch is much easier to be trained from many interesting practises. There are some tips in the recognization of intervals and chords.

T3--Some thoughts on perfect pitch

Most people go out of the tune when they are singing and they never come back. There are several suggerstions to help to solve this problem from the experience of me, my friends and students.

T4--Some thoughts on warm-up

For the professional pianists and opera singers, they can even perform well when they are sick or drunk. However, most people have to do some warm-up to make their perfances do not mess up, which is necessary and meaningful. I will show you some of my methods in warming myself up and share some of my original etudes.

T5--Some thoughts on reciting the scores

Both reciting the articles or poems from the textbooks and remembering the music scores are of great difficulty, but there are some tips to do some help, and make you a better performer.

T6--Some thoughts on voice leading

The excellent voice leading can always make you feel that "This must please the God and I'll definately be saved.", which is common in the churches. The basic rules of harmony in classical music are strict and logical, ensuring the music be harmonic. Some interesting stories about my composing learning may give you some inspirations.

T7--Some thoughts on conducting choirs

A choir consists of dozens of amateurs is always annoying, but you can still learn a lot from the challenges. I have conducted over ten times with different amateur choirs and maybe there are some experiences are worthy of reference.

T8--Some thoughts on piano tuning

When you observe the piano carefully, you will find that instrument is very elaborate and scientific. I think that the structure may do some help in playing and ear training. You may touch the piano keys with more thinking after reading this article.

T9--Some thoughts on the resonance in singing

For beginners to learn Bel Canto, it is confusing for them to make their sound "big and bright and thick and powerful." Their essential problem is to find the correct position of resonance. I'll tell you some of my methods to conquer this abstract definition.

T10--Some thoughts on pronunciation in singing

The proper pronounciation can make your singing been accepted by more audiences. All the vowels, consonants and the alveolar trills should be treated equally carefully. I can give you my suggestion in practising, which may also help in the learning of a foreign language .
