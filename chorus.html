Teacher

This page shows some of my scientific ideas from my teaching.
I might add a new idea whenever I weave one.
I think they are interesting.

T1--Piano training model

There should be a model to adjust the training targets for learners and do the supervision to help them learn how to play, and the specific targets of the model can be designed as follows.
1. Evaluate the performer's strengths and weaknesses from a few dimentions like:
a) loudness
b) speed
c) coordination
d) number of wrong notes
e) etc.
2. Design homework for the performer to enhance the weknesses and make the strengths stronger as well.

T2--Piano playing model

There are several features in human's performances listed as follows:
1. Human may have their own recreation in playing the scores.
2. Human's rhythms may be more casual and more imperfect. For example, the trills played in both crescendo and rita.
We could design a performing model based on such facts rather than just set the parameters of the pitches, tempos, etc.

T3--Human hand model

The existing piano playing programs do not consider such factors:
1. The coordination of human's left hand and right hand varied according to how long they practised.
2. Human may play some wrong notes due to the limitation of their hands.
3. The timber of notes are affected by how fingers touch the keys.
We could design a model cased on the structures of human's hands to simulate a more realistic piano performance. Maybe we need to coorporate with a med school.

T4--Piano difficulty model


We can give an objective score to describe how difficult a piano piece is.
We have GiantMIDI-Piano Dataset which can be used to train the difficulty evaluation model. We can use the unsupervised learning to cluster the performance in similar difficulty and give them labels, and we can give some examples:
1. The speed for Etudes in more than 1000 notes per minute can be discribed as difficult, which can be a rule-based algorithm.
2. The variation among the key-touching in a piece. There should be a catagory for different key-touching methods.
3. etc.
The comprehensive score for different dimentions could be illustrated in a chart.

T5--Pronunciation training model

For beginners in learning vocal, they may feel difficult to control the breathes, the resonance positions, the vibrados, etc. It is unrealistic to model all the listed and make the model just replace a vocal teacher. However, maybe some of the listed can be modeled to do some help.
1. Recognize the rhythm of reading the lyrics, and make it follow the accompaniment strictly.
2. Check the pronunciations for each phonome in the lyrics.
Besides correcting the pronunciation in singing, the model can also help in learning performance, poem recitation, etc.
